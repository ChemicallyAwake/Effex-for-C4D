POP::Weak Spring

Parameters
POP::Weak Spring

The weak spring has its magic in generating advantageous particle distributions. Particles are pushed away (as if a weak spring is inbetween them) from each other. This results in a more lively motion, in less surface noise (which the FLIP solver algorithm suffers from over time) and last but not least in conserving liquid volume!

If particles have a mass or particle density property attached these property values are used in the calculations. But they are not required.
If you are filling these properties yourself you should know what you are doing or at least be cautious about it.
