POP::Foam/Spray/Bubbles

Parameters Unified Foam Spray
POP::Foam/Spray/Bubbles

The foam, Spray & Bubbles node is a highly sophisticated operator & emitter of diffuse particles for large scale scenarios.

Diffuse particles is an expression for particles that seem moving around randomly or visually appear diffuse when seen from a normal or far range. Just think of a breaking wave at a cliff.

Note: That's why these secondary particles are stored in the Diffuse subgroup type of a Particle Group.

Foam, Spray and Bubbles are considered diffuse particles. We define these as follows:

1. Spray particles are flying in mid-air
2. Foam particles are on the liquid surface
3. Bubble particles are moving inside a liquid body.

In order to distinguish between these 3 diffuse particle types a particle carries an attribute namely: foam, spray or bubble.

Note: It's the same for thin sheet particles. They are of type Liquid, but they additionally have the 'sheet' attribute.
